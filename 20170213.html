When plating gold with a photoresist mask, we had problem with adhesion. **Photoresist in general adhere poorly to noble metals like Ag, Au**. Al and Ti are the most favorable metals in term of adhesion. Also **After HF removal of SiO2, the adhesion strongly depends on the completeness of removal**. If the surface is F free, it is passivated with H. Otherwise the remaining SiO2 forms compound with F and can only be restored at 700degC. [SiO2] (http://www.microchemicals.com/technical_information/resist_adhesion.pdf) In general it's better to avoid using HF to remove exposing Ti with photoresist mask as HF diffuses through the resist film.
