When plating gold with a photoresist mask, we had problem with adhesion. **Photoresist in general adhere poorly to noble metals like Ag, Au**. Al and Ti are the most favorable metals in term of adhesion. Also **After HF removal of SiO2, the adhesion strongly depends on the completeness of removal**. If the surface is F free, it is passivated with H. Otherwise the remaining SiO2 forms compound with F and can only be restored at 700degC. [[ref1]](http://www.microchemicals.com/technical_information/resist_adhesion.pdf) In general it's better to avoid using HF to remove exposing Ti with photoresist mask as HF diffuses through the resist film. I don't recommend Al despite its good adhesion. Al etching is highly exothermic, an (inevitable, since isotropic etching) under-etching of the resist mask causes local heating (increased etch rate) and super-proportional under etching of the mask as a consequence, if no agitation is performed. [[ref2]](http://www.microchemicals.de/secret/etching.pdf) Ti can be used if a proper dry etch is available. The typical etchants are 1. HF, 2. nitric acid + ammonium fluoride. Both attacks resist. An RIE etch with 200W RF power, 12sccm CF4 + 38sccm Ar will remove >10nm Ti in 30s. Cr is yet another option as removing cr does not involve any HF or nitric acid. A typical etchant is Ce(NH4)2(NO3)6 + CH3COOH. The reaction is very fast, do dilute to control the rate. SiO2 is interesting because Ti Prime is designed to work with it! It can be removed using RIE but no wet etch is recommended as it uses HF.
