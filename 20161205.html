loading effect in DSE - design considerations

DSE has loading effect, according to the model of Mogab [http://jes.ecsdl.org/content/124/8/1262][http://lib.tkk.fi/Diss/2005/isbn9513864367/article2.pdf], a good design should have an etchable area <20%, feature size should be <2mm.

Michigan Method Recipe

In the DSE tool someone used the method introduced by Michigan U [http://wims.eecs.umich.edu/publications/papers/OwenNajafiMEMSJan2012.pdf] which can give us a high aspect ratio by varying parameters in the process.

Au etch and redeposition in ICP

A through study[http://www.eee.metu.edu.tr/~tayfuna/papers/akinj37JMM_AuEtch.pdf] of Ti/ Au deposition in ICP etching. Their metal system is very like ours: Si-SiNx-Cr-Au-PR. Some interesting results from them: 1)They showed that a thin Ti layer of 7nm can help eliminate the byproduct of Cl2 etching. Ti is a hardmask here. 2)The byproduct of Cl2 ICP are AuClx and Au particles 3)A higher RF platen power is preferred - their recipe is Cl2 15sccm Ar 5sccm Pressure 2mTorr RF Coil 600W RF platen 250W Table temperature 20degC 4)They also tried different resist however since they are americans theres no help to us on which resist is better.
