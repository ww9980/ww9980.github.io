SINR 3570

SINR 3570 is a novel Siloxane-based, photo-patternable packaging material developed by Shin-Etsu MicroSi. It is known to be a good wafer bonding material and can be used for flattening dielectric layer in MEMS applications.

Like SU-8 and BCB (bencocyclobutene), this material allows adhesive bonding which features low bonding temperature, ability to join different wafer materials, and high tolerance to surface topography. Typically they are low-k materials.

SINR 3570 series is based on 3170 series thus have a similar property. It's negative tone, and can easily achieve 1~25um thickness. A SINR3570-PF20's nominal (i.e. coated at 5k rpm) thickness is 20um.

Here is a guide on using SINR3570 series, some data from a NPIL paper on bonding.

Coating

Although it is suppose to be nominal thickness at 5k rpm, often it is not.

  • SINR3570-PF20@20um@2800 rpm@20s
  • SINR3570-PF3@2um@4000 rpm@20s

Soft bake

-SINR3570-PF20@100@5min -SINR3570-PF3@100@1min

Post exposure bake

-SINR3570-PF20@120@2min -SINR3570-PF3@110@2min

Development

-SINR3570-PF20@IPA@4min -SINR3570-PF3@IPA@2x1min

To Remove

  • before exposure: rinse with acetone then IPA
  • after exposure: FNA or RIE (using CF4+O2, pressure=100mT, flowrate=12: 50 sccm respectively, 5min removes all 5um resist)

Bonding

-SINR3570-PF20@180∘180∘C@5min@1.5MPa+Post cure@180∘180∘C@60min
